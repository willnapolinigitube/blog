## 📚 8 Rules That Will Change How You See Love

Jay shares 8 practical, simple yet profound principles. He writes from the ground, not from above — with humor, warmth, and real-life experience backed by spiritual practice.

No preaching, just heart. It's like having a deep conversation with a wise friend over evening tea.

---

### Rule 1. Love Yourself First. There's No Way Around It

Before building a palace of love, you need to clean out the mental garage.

Shetty insists: if you don't respect yourself, your partner will mirror that back to you.

Self-love isn't bubble baths and Netflix (though those are nice). It's the ability to tell yourself: *"I'm worthy, even when I'm not perfect."*

{% include tip-card.html icon="✨" title="Example:" text="Imagine you weren't invited to a party. Old you panics: 'I'm worthless!' New you makes tea with honey and watches comfort shows without guilt. New perspective — new reactions." %}

---

### Rule 2. Love Isn't Magic — It's Practice

We love to think love is like in the movies: boom — butterflies in your stomach.

But Jay says: love is a habit, a workout, like going to the gym.

Every day — small choices toward the other person. Saying "I'm sorry," making coffee when you'd rather sleep in.

{% include tip-card.html icon="💖" title="Tip:" text="Want stronger love? Do small kind things without being asked. Every single day. Even when you don't feel like it. Love lives in the details." %}

---

### Rule 3. You're Not a Half. You're a Whole Pie

Shetty breaks the myth of the "other half."

Love isn't two missing pieces fitting together — it's two whole people choosing each other. You're already complete.

Relationships are like a club you join, not a rescue mission.

{% include tip-card.html icon="🌸" title="Tip:" text="Go on dates with yourself. Seriously. Movie, walk, café. Love your own company — and others will too. You're not boring to be around." %}

---

### Rule 4. Learn to Listen, Not to Win

**Story:**

A man came to a wise teacher and said:

"My wife doesn't listen to me. I talk, and she stays silent."

The teacher smiled:

"Do you listen to understand, or to respond?"

The man thought about it.

"Listen as if you want to see their soul. Then they'll hear you too."

Listening means putting your phone down and stopping the mental to-do list. It means not interrupting, even when you're dying to speak. That's respect.

---

### Rule 5. Conflicts Aren't Enemies — They're Teachers

Even Harry and Hermione fought.

What matters is how you come out of it. Not "winning," but "understanding." Not "holding grudges," but "talking it through."

{% include tip-card.html icon="🫂" title="Tip:" text="Next time an argument starts, ask: 'What are you really afraid of right now?' Sometimes behind the yelling is the fear of being rejected. First comes the pain, then the closeness." %}

---

### Rule 6. Grow Together, Not Against Each Other

If one person hits the gym and the other hits the fridge, it'll be tough.

Growing together means being curious, supporting, inspiring — not playing tug-of-war.

As the saying goes: *"You can't row the boat if only one person is paddling."*

{% include tip-card.html icon="🧘♂️" title="Example:" text="Instead of 'You and your yoga again,' try 'Can you show me how to breathe properly?' Maybe you'll find your zen too? Curiosity brings you closer." %}

---

### Rule 7. Love Is a Choice You Make Every Single Day

Even on the day you want to throw your partner's toothbrush out the window.

Love isn't always a feeling, but it's always a decision: to stay, to forgive, to care.

Every day — you hit "refresh" and keep going.

---

### Rule 8. Let Go with Love

Sometimes love means walking away.

Shetty says: relationships can be temporary, but the lessons are forever.

Don't hold onto someone who isn't holding onto you. Letting go isn't losing — it's wisdom.

---

## 🏆 Champion's Challenge: "Love Journal" — 5 Minutes of Mindfulness

Every evening for a week, write in your notebook:

- What are you grateful for in your relationship today?
- What did you do to show love?
- What do you want to improve tomorrow?

The effect is like meditation, only more enjoyable.
